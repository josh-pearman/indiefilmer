## Scene List

The scenes page shows every scene in your project. Each scene displays its scene number, INT/EXT designation, DAY/NIGHT setting, page count, and a short synopsis. You can filter and sort scenes by various criteria.

## Scene Details

Click a scene to open its detail page. Here you can edit:

- **Scene number** — e.g. "1", "2A", "14"
- **INT/EXT** — interior or exterior
- **DAY/NIGHT** — time of day for the scene
- **Location** — which location this scene takes place at
- **Page count** — in eighths (standard script format)
- **Synopsis** — a brief description of the scene

## Cast Assignment

From the scene detail page, assign cast members who appear in this scene. This drives the cast list on call sheets — when a scene is scheduled on a shoot day, its cast members are automatically included on that day's call sheet.

## Production Tags

Tag scenes with production notes that affect planning:

- **Stunts** — requires stunt coordination
- **VFX** — visual effects needed in post
- **SFX** — practical special effects on set
- **Permit** — location permit required
- **Animals** — animal wrangler needed
- **Vehicles** — picture vehicles in scene

These tags help you plan resources and flag any special requirements during scheduling.
